Block 1 — Vinyasa yoga flow (12 min · 3 rounds)
Move with the breath. Inhale to open, exhale to deepen. 3 full rounds, no rest between.
① Mountain → Forward fold → Half lift
1 breath each
Stand tall — chest open, shoulders back and down, crown lifting. This posture is the goal permanently. Exhale fold from hips. Inhale lift chest halfway — back flat like a table.
② Low lunge (Crescent)
5 breaths each side
Step one foot back, back knee on floor, arms sweep overhead. Hip square, chest open. The deepest hip flexor stretch available — releases the front thigh and slims it over time. Alternate sides each round.
③ Warrior II → Reverse Warrior → Extended side angle
3 breaths each
Warrior II: front knee over ankle, arms wide. Reverse: back arm down back leg, front arm sweeps overhead — feel the entire side waist open. Extended side angle: forearm to front thigh, top arm extends long — works the oblique, inner thigh, and arm isometrically all at once.
④ Plank → Slow lower (4 sec) → Cobra → Downward dog
1 breath each
Plank: wrists under shoulders. Lower over 4 full seconds — elbows back, not out. Cobra: press chest up. Downward dog: hips high, heels toward floor, pedal feet. The 4-sec lower is the arm-toning moment — never rush it.
⑤ Walk forward → Roll up to Mountain
3 breaths
Walk feet to hands, fold, roll up vertebra by vertebra. One round complete — begin next immediately.

Block 2 — Full body mobility + posture (8 min)
Posture correction alone visually slims the shoulders, lifts the chest, and makes the waist look smaller — before any fat loss at all.
Wall posture press
3 × 45 sec
Stand with back flat against wall — heels, glutes, upper back, and head all touching. Arms at 90° (goalpost shape), press gently into the wall. Hold. This is what correct posture feels like — the body re-learns it with daily practice. Most people feel this intensely in the upper back because it is the weakest area.
💡 Daily posture work done consistently reshapes how the body looks in clothes more than almost any exercise. Rounded shoulders add apparent width. Correcting them removes it.
Thoracic extension over rolled towel
2 min
Roll a towel tightly, place it between the shoulder blades horizontally, lie back on it with arms overhead. Breathe deeply and let gravity open the upper back. Directly counteracts the hunching that creates the broad-shoulder appearance.
Chest opener hold
2 min
Standing, clasp hands behind back, squeeze shoulder blades together, lift chest. Hold while breathing. Do every Monday and Sunday. Builds the open-chest silhouette passively over 4 months.

Block 3 — Beginner calisthenics foundations (8 min)
Calisthenics = body control and leanness. These beginner moves are the direct foundation of every advanced skill in the plan.
Dead hang
3 × 20 sec
Grip a door frame or any sturdy overhead bar, arms straight, hang freely. Decompresses the spine, lengthens the torso, builds grip and shoulder strength needed for everything ahead. The most beginner-friendly calisthenics exercise — zero injury risk when done correctly.
💡 Daily hanging decompresses the spine and improves posture visibly within 2–3 weeks. Makes the torso look longer and leaner.
Scapular push-up
3 × 12
High plank, arms completely straight. Without bending elbows, let the chest sink between the shoulders (blades come together), then push the floor away (blades spread wide). This tiny movement trains the serratus anterior — essential for handstand balance and the "athletic lean" silhouette.
Plank hold
3 × 40 sec
High plank, wrists under shoulders, one straight line. Breathe into the sides of the rib cage. Foundation of every calisthenics skill ahead.
Side plank hold
3 × 30 sec each side
On hand or forearm, body in one straight line. Hold still. Works the oblique — the muscle that carves the visible hourglass curve on the waist.
Vacuum hold
10 × 20 sec
Stand, exhale fully, draw belly button to spine as deeply as possible, hold 20 sec. Every Monday, every morning. The transverse abdominis physically pulls the waistline inward with consistent practice.

Block 2 — Hip unlocking sequence (15 min)
The hips are the key to splits, wider hip appearance, slimmer inner thighs, and graceful movement. Never rush these holds — breathing and patience do more than forcing.
90/90 hip stretch
2 min each side
Sit with both legs bent at 90° — front leg in front, back leg to the side. Both shins flat on the floor. Sit tall, lean forward over front shin. This position opens both the internal and external hip rotator at the same time — it is one of the most complete hip openers available.
💡 The 90/90 is used by professional dancers and gymnasts specifically because it opens every part of the hip joint at once. It will be uncomfortable at first — that is the hip releasing its chronic tightness.
Pigeon pose
2 min each side
One shin roughly parallel to the front of the mat (it will be angled at first — that is fine), back leg extended. Fold forward over front shin gradually. Activates the hip external rotators — the muscles that make hips look rounder and wider from behind.
Lizard pose
90 sec each side
Low lunge with both hands inside front foot on the floor. Sink hips toward the mat. The deepest hip flexor opener available — directly accelerates split and kick progress. Can lower to forearms when ready.
Butterfly stretch
2 min
Soles of feet together, press knees toward floor with elbows. Inner groin release — essential for inner thigh slimming and middle split progress.
Frog pose
3 min hold
Hands and knees, slide knees as wide as possible, feet in line with knees (toes pointing out). Sink hips toward the floor. Exhale and sink deeper each breath. The most powerful inner thigh and hip opener available — the discomfort is the adductor muscle releasing. By week 4 this feels normal.

Block 3 — Ballet barre basics (10 min)
Ballet barre work creates the long, lean, feminine body type. No experience needed — these are absolute beginner movements. Use a chair back as your barre.
Ballet standing posture
Hold 30 sec, feel it first
Stand beside chair, lightly hold the back. Feet in first position (heels together, toes turned out to 45°). Shoulders back and down, core gently engaged, chin level. This is ballet posture — it is also perfect everyday posture. Feel the length from heel to crown before any movement begins.
Tendus — front, side, back
8 each direction, each side
From first position, slide one foot along the floor — front, then side, then back — pointing the foot fully at the end. Return. The foot never leaves the floor. This is the most foundational ballet movement — trains the entire leg to move gracefully from the hip and builds the long inner thigh line that makes legs look elegant.
Demi plié in first position
3 × 16 slow reps
Feet in first position. Bend both knees outward over toes (never let knees fall inward), lower until heels start to lift, then rise. Keep the back completely upright — do not lean forward. The most fundamental ballet movement for inner thigh toning and hip turnout.
Battement derrière (back kick)
3 × 12 each side
Hold chair, extend one leg straight behind at hip height, point the foot. Lower and repeat. This is a ballet version of the donkey kick — but done with a pointed foot and straight knee, it also trains the full back line of the leg from glute to ankle.
Port de bras (arm movement)
10 slow sequences
Stand free of chair. Arms start in low position (slightly in front of hips, slightly rounded). Slowly raise to middle position (in front at belly height), then to high position (overhead, slightly rounded). Lower back down. Slow, intentional, graceful. This trains the arm muscles from shoulder to fingertip — the movements that create the lean elegant arm appearance over months.

Block 4 — Splits + backbend progression (12 min)
Only attempt after the full hip opening sequence above — cold splits cause injury. Go to the point of strong stretch, never sharp pain.
Front split — right leg
3 min hold
Use the wall for balance. Slide front leg forward, back leg back. Week 1: ~50–60% depth. Week 4: 80–90%. Week 8: flat or nearly flat. Never force past a sharp sensation — a strong pull is correct, sharp pain is not.
Front split — left leg
3 min hold
The non-dominant side is almost always stiffer. Equal time — do not cheat this side or one leg will always lag behind.
Middle split progression
3 min hold
Face the wall, slide feet sideways along the wall. Keep torso upright. This is the hardest split — progress is slower. Use the frog pose to prepare it and build from here week by week.
Bridge hold — beginner to advanced
30 sec × 4
Beginner: glute bridge only (hips off floor, hands flat by sides). Intermediate: full bridge on hands and feet, arms bent. Advanced: full bridge with straight arms. Opens chest, builds back strength, improves posture. Move to the next level only when current level feels comfortable.

Block 1 — Gymnastics fear-free skill ladder (20 min)
Stay on each step until it feels completely comfortable and safe before moving to the next. There is no timeline pressure — taking 4 weeks on step 1 is correct if that is what the body needs. Each step directly prepares the next.
🟢 Step 1 — Hollow body + arch hold (ALWAYS done first)
30 sec × 4 each
Hollow: on back, lift shoulders and straight legs, arms overhead, lower back flat. Arch: face down, lift chest and legs, arms forward. These are never skipped — they are the core of every single gymnastics skill. If you cannot hold hollow for 30 sec, every other step will feel unstable. Build this first.
💡 Every professional gymnast does hollow and arch daily. They are not beginner exercises — they are the foundation every skill is built on. Do not rush past them.
🟢 Step 2 — Scapular push-ups + shoulder shrugs in plank
3 × 12
In plank: let chest sink between shoulders (blades together), then push floor away (blades spread). Then: in plank, press arms as straight as possible and shrug shoulders slightly toward ears. These build the shoulder awareness and strength that make handstand balance possible — and they are completely safe.
🟡 Step 3 — Pike push-up (handstand shape prep)
3 × 8
From downward dog position, bend elbows and lower the top of the head toward the floor between the hands, then push back up. Arms track back not out. This is the exact shoulder movement used in a handstand — but completely safe with feet on the floor. Only move to step 4 when these feel strong.
🟡 Step 4 — Elevated pike (feet on wall or chair)
3 × 30 sec hold
Hands on floor, feet on a chair or low step, hips raised in a pike position. Hold and feel the shoulder load. This is the closest preparation to an actual handstand — the body position and weight distribution are identical, but there is zero fall risk. When this feels normal, proceed to step 5.
🟠 Step 5 — Wall handstand kick-up (facing wall)
8 kick attempts
Face the wall, hands 15–20 cm from the wall. Kick one leg up while the other follows — both feet land on the wall. Hold as long as comfortable. The wall is directly in front so there is zero risk of falling backward. Only attempt this after steps 1–4 feel solid. Do not rush to this step.
🟠 Step 6 — Cartwheel from standing (when ready — weeks 4–8+)
× 5 each side
Only attempt when: you can hold hollow body for 45 sec, pike push-ups feel easy, and you feel completely calm about it. Stand with arms overhead, step to the side, place one hand then the other on the floor with legs going over sideways. If unsure, do a side roll on the floor first (tuck and roll sideways). There is no deadline for this step.
💡 Fear of cartwheels is almost always about not knowing where your body is in space, not about lack of strength. The hollow body work and wall handstands teach exactly that — by the time you attempt a cartwheel from step 6, the body already knows what to do.

Fear-free gymnastics skill ladder
No fixed timeline. Stay at each level until it feels comfortable and safe before moving to the next. Rushing causes injury and slows overall progress. Each level directly prepares the one above it.
🟢 Level 1 — Core foundations
Weeks 1–4+, always done first
Hollow body hold, arch hold, scapular push-ups, forearm plank. Never "finished" — done first even at advanced levels. Master level: hollow 45 sec, arch 45 sec clean.
🟢 Level 2 — Shoulder prep
Weeks 2–6+
Pike push-ups + elevated pike hold (feet on chair). Teaches exact handstand shoulder mechanics with zero fall risk. Master level: 3×10 pike push-ups, 3×45 sec elevated pike.
🟡 Level 3 — Wall handstand facing wall
Weeks 4–10+
Hands 15–20 cm from wall, kick feet up to wall. Zero backward fall risk. Builds wrist loading + shoulder alignment + spatial awareness. Master level: 30 sec hold.
🟡 Level 4 — Cartwheel
Weeks 6–12+
Side roll on floor first → slow lunge cartwheel → full standing. Fear disappears when the body knows where it is in space — levels 1–3 teach exactly that. Master level: clean both sides.
🟠 Level 5 — Freestanding handstand
Weeks 9–16+
Kick up away from wall — 2–3 sec is progress. The hollow body work makes balance possible. Master level: 10+ sec hold.
🔴 Level 6 — Round-off + back walkover
Weeks 12–16+
Round-off: cartwheel snapping feet together on landing. Back walkover: walk hands down wall into bridge then through. Phase 3–4 goals — they come naturally from everything built before.
